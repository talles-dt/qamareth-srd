## 1. CORE PHILOSOPHY

Qamareth is a narrative-first RPG where **fiction determines mechanics, not the other way around.**

### What Qamareth IS
- A game where characters are defined by **stories, not stats**
- A resolution engine where **conditions change what you can do, not how well you do it**
- A beat economy where **rhythm replaces initiative**
- A spiritual system where **internal struggles create dramatic choices, not spreadsheet penalties**
- A world where **magic is invitation, not command**

### What Qamareth IS NOT
- No hit points, spell slots, levels, or classes
- No +N/-N numeric bonuses or penalties
- No advantage/disadvantage, DC checks, or passive defense
- No short rests, long rests, bonus actions, or grid squares
- No D&D terminology or mechanical shortcuts

### The Golden Rule
**If a rule makes the game feel like a spreadsheet, rewrite it.**
**If a rule forces a player to choose between good roleplay and good mechanics, rewrite it.**
**If a rule can't be explained in one sentence of fiction, rewrite it.**

## 2. ATTRIBUTES — THE 15 PILLARS

Every character has 15 attributes organized into 5 Pillars. Each Pillar represents a fundamental way of engaging with the world.

### The Five Pillars

| Pillar | Governs |
|---|---|
| **Espírito** (Spirit) | Faith, conviction, connection to the divine, moral center |
| **Presença** (Presence) | Social weight, emotional influence, authority, charisma |
| **Engenho** (Ingenuity) | Analysis, strategy, perception, technical understanding |
| **Vigor** (Vigor) | Physical power, endurance, speed, resilience |
| **Discernimento** (Discernment) | Intuition, insight, reading the room, hidden knowledge |

### The Fifteen Attributes

Each Pillar contains 3 sub-attributes:

| Pillar | Attribute | Governs |

| **Espírito** | Fé (Faith) | Trust in the divine, spiritual resilience, liturgical power |
| | Convicção (Conviction) | Adherence to personal truth, resistance to ideological pressure |

## 3. DISCIPLINES — THE 19 TRADITIONS

Disciplines represent trained competence in a specific tradition. They determine **what size die you roll** when using that Discipline.

### Discipline Ratings and Die Types

| Rating | Die Type | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | d4 | Initiate — exposed to this tradition, can attempt it |
| **1** | d6 | Practiced — regular user, dependable under normal conditions |
| **2** | d8 | Proficient — notable within a community of practitioners |
| **3** | d10 | Expert — sought out, this is what you're known for |
| **4-5** | d12 | Master — one of the finest; shaped by and shaping this tradition |

### The 19 Disciplines

**Combat (5):**
- **Lâmina** — bladed weapons technique, precision cutting, parry rhythm
- **Impacto** — blunt, heavy, percussive weapons; breaking force
- **Alcance** — ranged weapons, zone control, projectile timing
- **Defesa** — active defense, reaction economy, shield work
- **Movimento** — repositioning, zone manipulation, footwork

**Magical (5):**
- **Voz** — voice-based magical execution, singing, chanting, spoken invocation
- **Instrumento** — instrument-based execution, resonant manipulation
- **Escrita** — notation-based execution, runic writing, scrollcraft
- **Gesto** — somatic execution, dance, hand signs, physical invocation
- **Coletivo** — collective amplification, choir magic, ensemble resonance

**Social (5):**
- **Retórica** — argument, persuasion, public performance, formal debate
- **Negociação** — deal-making, leverage, contractual interaction
- **Reputação** — faction standing, social positioning, network access
- **Intimidação** — pressure, dominance, social combat through fear
- **Leitura** — reading a person, situation, or room accurately

**Knowledge (4):**
- **Grimório** — grimoire reading, tradition reconstruction, magical identification
- **Tática** — battlefield analysis, strategic planning, threat assessment
- **História Harmônica** — lost traditions, political history of music, cultural memory
- **Magi-tech** — operating and understanding magi-tech devices, reverse engineering

## 4. RESOLUTION ENGINE

### The Core Mechanic

```
Pool = Attribute sub-attribute rating (1-5 dice)
Die = Discipline rating determines die type (d4-d12)

Roll Pool dice of the Discipline's die type.
Count SUCCESSES (each die showing 7+).
Compare to the action's THRESHOLD.
```

### Thresholds

| Difficulty | Threshold | When |
|---|---|---|
| Routine | 1+ | Everyday tasks, no meaningful risk |
| Standard | 2+ | Challenging but achievable for trained characters |
| Difficult | 3+ | Requires genuine competence |
| Heroic | 4+ | Pushes the limits of mortal capability |
| Mythic | 5+ | Near-impossible; requires mastery + luck + narrative alignment |

### Extended Actions

Some actions require **sustained effort** across multiple compassos or scenes. For extended actions:

```
Set a SUCCESS TARGET based on complexity (3-10+ successes).
Each compasso, roll and add successes to the cumulative total.
When cumulative successes ≥ target, the action succeeds.
```

This is the only time a "minimum roll level" applies — not for single actions, only for extended ones.

### Exploding Dice

When any die rolls its **maximum face value** (4 on d4, 6 on d6, 8 on d8, 10 on d10, 12 on d12), **reroll it** and add the new result's successes. If the reroll is also a max, reroll again. This represents moments of inspired brilliance.

### Margin of Success → Conditions, Not Modifiers

When you exceed the threshold, the margin of success creates **narrative advantages expressed as conditions**:

| Excess Successes | Effect |
|---|---|
| +1 over threshold | Minor advantage — you gain positioning, information, or a small opening |
| +2 over threshold | Significant advantage — create a beneficial condition for yourself or an ally |
| +3+ over threshold | Major advantage — create a condition for an enemy, shift the scene's tempo, or achieve a secondary objective |

Conditions created this way are **fictional states with mechanical triggers** (see §7), not numeric modifiers.

### Failure

Failure doesn't mean nothing happens. It means **something happens that complic the situation**. The GM introduces a condition, a complication, or an unexpected consequence. The fiction continues — just not the way the player intended.

### Critical Failure (All Dice Below 7)

When every die in the pool shows below 7, the action fails **spectacularly**. The GM applies a significant condition to the character (Desritmado, Exposto, or a custom condition based on context).

## 5. BEAT ECONOMY

### The Compasso

A combat scene is divided into **COMPASSOS** (rounds). Each compasso has **4 BEATS**.

Beats represent the rhythm of conflict. Every action costs beats. Every character acts within the compasso.

### Speed Classes

| Speed | Beat Cost | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Reação** (Reaction) | 0 beats | One reactive action per compasso. Interrupts, parries, dodges. |
| **Ligeiro** (Light) | 1 beat | Quick actions: feints, shouts, minor strikes, repositioning. |
| **Médio** (Standard) | 2 beats | Normal actions: standard attacks, defensive maneuvers, most executions. |
| **Pesado** (Heavy) | 3-4 beats | Devastating actions: decisive strikes, grand executions, scene-changing moves. |

### How a Compasso Works

1. **All characters declare their intended actions** at the start of the compasso.
2. **Actions resolve in speed order:** Reação (0) → Ligeiro (1) → Médio (2) → Pesado (3-4).
3. **Within the same speed class**, the GM decides order based on fiction (who has the initiative narratively, who was poised to act, etc.).
4. **After all declared actions resolve**, the compasso ends. A new compasso begins with the next 4 beats.

### Tempo Livre (Free Beat)

Certain actions cost **0 beats** if they are narratively aligned with:
- The character's **Motivo de Origem**
- A **Dominant Passion** (6-8+) being acted upon
- A **Scar Condition** trigger that the character embraces

The GM decides what qualifies. This is not a D&D bonus action — it's the rhythm of who you are. Examples:
- A character with a protective Motivo can interpose as Tempo Livre.
- A character with Ira at 6-8 can shout a warning as Tempo Livre (confrontation is their nature).
- A character whose Scar triggers on fire can act with heightened clarity as Tempo Livre when surrounded by flame.

### Reaction Economy

Each character gets **one Reaction per compasso** (0 beats). Reactions include:
- Parrying an incoming attack
- Dodging to safety
- Shouting a warning
- Activating a Scar Condition
- Interrupting with a Ligeiro action (costs 1 beat from your next compasso)

Reactions are narrative, not mechanical triggers. The fiction must support the reaction.

## 6. VITALITY POOL & RESILIENCE STATES

### Vitality Pool (VP)

Every character has a **Vitality Pool** — a reserve of narrative resilience. VP is **not hit points**. It is the character's capacity to absorb pressure before breaking.

**VP = Resistência (Vigor) + Firmeza (Discernimento)** — the sum of these two sub-attribute ratings, typically 4-10.

VP works as a **condition buffer**:
- Taking damage doesn't subtract from VP — it **adds conditions** that consume VP capacity.
- Each condition occupies 1 point of VP capacity.
- When VP is full of conditions, the character is at maximum pressure.
- VP recovers through **Compasso de Pausa** and **Retiro** (see §17).

### Resilience States

Every character exists in one of four states at any time:

| State | Conditions | Mechanical Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Claro** (Clear) | 0-1 active conditions | Full action set. All options available. |
| **Pressionado** (Pressured) | 2-3 active conditions | Heavy actions cost +1 beat. Reactions limited to 1 per compasso. |
| **Comprometido** (Compromised) | 4+ active conditions | Reactions reduced to 0 per compasso. Cannot take Pesado actions. |
| **Quebrado** (Broken) | Scene-ending condition | Character cannot continue in the scene. Not necessarily death — could be retreat, capture, surrender, or collapse. |

### Entering a New State

A character transitions between states based on the **number of active conditions** they carry. When a condition is cleared (through healing, narrative resolution, or time), the character may move back to a lighter state.

### The Golden Rule of VP

**VP is not spent. VP is capacity.** It determines how many conditions you can carry before becoming Comprometido. Healing removes conditions, which frees VP capacity.

## 7. CONDITIONS

Conditions are the primary mechanical expression of pressure in Qamareth. They change **what you can do**, not how well you do it.

### Core Condition List

| Condition | Type | Effect | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Desritmado** | Combat | Next action costs +1 beat | Interrupted action, failed timing |
| **Exposto** | Combat | Next hit against you is undefended | Lost reaction, flanked, caught flat-footed |
| **Atordoado** | Combat | Skip next compasso reaction | Decisive strike, sonic/magical shock |
| **Sangrando** | Combat | VP capacity reduced by 1 | Specific weapon condition, grievous wound |
| **Imobilizado** | Combat | Cannot change zones | Grapple, terrain, magical restraint |
| **Dominado** | Combat | Opponent has beat 0 option against you | Opponent achieved overwhelming advantage |
| **Pesado** | Combat | All movement costs +1 beat | Armor cost, heavy blow landed |
| **Dissonância** | Magic | +2 beat cost on next execution | Magical interruption, execution failure |
| **Harmônico** | Magic | Next collective execution: +1 scale tier | Perfect execution, Transcendência |
| **Saturado** | Magic | Same-discipline executions cost +1 beat until cleared | Rapid successive executions |
| **Ressonante** | Magic | All executions in scene have raised threshold; Transcendências doubled | Scene-wide collective effect |
| **Silenciado** | Magic+Social | Cannot use Voice/Instrument disciplines; cannot speak in social scene | Medium destroyed, social Broken state |
| **Gula** | Passion | Hoarding, addiction, refusing to share | Passion of Gluttony triggered |
| **Luxúria** | Passion | Objectifying persons, manipulative seduction | Passion of Lust triggered |
| **Avareza** | Passion | Love of money/hoarding, fear of poverty | Passion of Avarice triggered |
| **Ira** | Passion | Wrath, revenge, refusing to forgive | Passion of Anger triggered |
| **Tristeza** | Passion | Despair, hopelessness, "the empire can never fall" | Passion of Sadness triggered |
| **Acídia** | Passion | Spiritual laziness, skipping duties, "noonday demon" | Passion of Acedia triggered |
| **Vainagloria** | Passion | Bragging, performing virtue for applause | Passion of Vainglory triggered |
| **Soberba** | Passion | Self-reliance, "I don't need anyone", God-Emperor's sin | Passion of Pride triggered |
| **Motif Overflow** | Magic | Exceeding motif capacity causes dissonance | Too many motifs in single execution |
| **Kenotic State** | Spiritual | True power through sacrificial love; victory through suffering | Achieved through kenosis practice |
| **Nepsis Focus** | Spiritual | Watchfulness grants advantage against logismoi | Prayer of the Heart practice (5 levels) |

### Creating New Conditions

The GM can create custom conditions for any situation. A condition should:
1. Have a **name** (poetic, evocative, thematically resonant)
2. Have a **clear mechanical effect** that changes behavior, not numbers
3. Have a **narrative trigger** (when it activates)
4. Have a **clear resolution path** (how it clears)

### Condition Stacking

A character can carry multiple conditions. When conditions stack:
- **Same condition twice:** The second instance upgrades the effect (e.g., Desritmado ×2 = next TWO actions cost +1 beat)
- **Conflicting conditions:** The more severe condition takes precedence
- **Scar Condition trigger:** Overrides all other conditions for that specific moment

## 8. SCAR CONDITIONS

Every character has one **Scar Condition** — a wound that is also a teacher. Scars are derived from **Question 2** of character creation ("What did you survive?").

### Structure

```
**Scar name:** [What the character calls it]
**Trigger:** [Specific narrative situation that activates it]
**Condition applied:** [Mechanical effect when triggered]
**Heightened access:** [What it lets them do that isn't normally possible]
**Resolution:** [How it is cleared — or if it ever is]
```

### Examples

#### Cinzas na Garganta (Ashes in the Throat)
- **Trigger:** When surrounded by fire or when someone questions your right to knowledge
- **Condition applied:** Silenciado — you cannot use Voice discipline and cannot speak for the duration of the scene
- **Heightened access:** You can read burned or damaged grimoires that would be illegible to others; smoke and flame reveal hidden text to you alone
- **Resolution:** Clears when you speak a truth that costs you something valuable, or when you share forbidden knowledge freely

#### O Peso dos Doze (Weight of the Twelve)
- **Trigger:** When you are responsible for others in combat and one of them falls
- **Condition applied:** Tristeza — you cannot take offensive actions until the fallen is avenged or recovered
- **Heightened access:** You gain Reação actions equal to the number of allies you've lost (up to 3); each represents a name you carry
- **Resolution:** Clears when you save someone else from the same fate, or when you speak all the names aloud in the presence of those who knew them

#### O Ouvido Partido (The Broken Ear)
- **Trigger:** When you hear the music of the Empire (imperial hymns, state ceremonies, propaganda broadcasts)
- **Condition applied:** Atordoado — skip next compasso reaction as memories flood back
- **Heightened access:** You can hear the "wrong notes" in imperial magic — identify hidden conditions, detect disguised Partituras, and see through harmonic illusions
- **Resolution:** Clears when you compose and perform a counter-melody that disrupts the imperial music in a public space

#### O Que Não Foi Dito (What Was Not Said)
- **Trigger:** When you witness an injustice and remain silent
- **Condition applied:** Vainagloria — you cannot use Retórica or Negociação until you confess the silence to someone affected
- **Heightened access:** You can sense unspoken truths in any conversation — lies glow, hidden motives cast shadows, and silence itself speaks to you
- **Resolution:** Clears when you speak the truth you withheld, regardless of the consequences

#### O Último Acorde (The Last Chord)
- **Trigger:** When you play the instrument or sing the melody of someone you've lost
- **Condition applied:** Soberba — you cannot accept help from anyone for the duration of the scene
- **Heightened access:** Your music reaches those who have also suffered loss — they hear it even at great distance, and it moves them to action
- **Resolution:** Clears when you play for someone who has not yet suffered loss, and teach them the melody

#### A Marca do Pântano (The Swamp's Mark)
- **Trigger:** When you are in water or mud, especially still water
- **Condition applied:** Imobilizado — the water remembers what you did; you cannot move from your position
- **Heightened access:** You can breathe underwater and see through murky depths; the swamp speaks to you, revealing hidden things and warning of approaching threats
- **Resolution:** Clears when you use the swamp's gift to save someone from drowning, even if it means revealing your secret

#### O Nome Apagado (The Erased Name)
- **Trigger:** When someone asks about your past or your family
- **Condition applied:** Silenciado — you cannot speak about your origins until the scene ends
- **Heightened access:** You can erase your presence from magical records, scrying attempts, and faction intelligence — you become a ghost in the system
- **Resolution:** Clears when you voluntarily reveal your true name to someone you trust, knowing the risk

#### A Dívida do Ferro (The Iron Debt)
- **Trigger:** When you are offered something for free or without cost
- **Condition applied:** Avareza — you cannot accept gifts; you must pay for everything or refuse
- **Heightened access:** You can sense the true cost of anything — the hidden price, the future obligation, the blood in the transaction
- **Resolution:** Clears when you accept a gift that has no hidden cost, and learn to receive without paying

### Priority Rule

When a Scar Condition triggers, it **takes priority over all other conditions** for that specific moment. If a character is Quebrado and their Scar triggers simultaneously, the Scar resolves first — and its heightened access may pull the character back from the brink, or push them further into the fire.

## 9. THE PASSION SYSTEM

The Passions are the **eight logismoi** — the root dispositions of the soul. Every character tracks all eight at all times.

### The Eight Passions

| Passion | Virtue | Theme |
|---|---|---|
| **Gula** (Gluttony) | Temperance | Disordered relationship with body, pleasure, consumption |
| **Luxúria** (Lust) | Chastity | Reducing persons to objects, manipulative seduction |
| **Avareza** (Avarice) | Generosity | Love of money/power, fear of poverty, hoarding |
| **Ira** (Anger) | Meekness | Wrath, revenge, refusing to forgive, desire for destruction |
| **Tristeza** (Sadness) | Joy | Despair, hopelessness, refusal of consolation |
| **Acídia** (Sloth) | Diligence | Spiritual laziness, boredom, the "noonday demon" |
| **Vainagloria** (Vainglory) | Humility | Bragging, performing virtue for applause, seeking praise |
| **Soberba** (Pride) | Humility | Self-sufficiency, "I don't need anyone", ultimate independence |

### The Chain

The Passions feed each other in the Evagrian Chain:
```
Gula → Luxúria → Avareza → Ira → Tristeza → Acídia → Vainagloria → Soberba → (back to) Gula
```

### Levels and Effects

| Level | Stage | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **0-2** | Natural | Latent. No mechanical effect. The passion exists but is quiet. |
| **3-5** | Struggle | **Compulsion Condition** active. When the Passion is triggered, you must either act on it OR make a test of willpower (Resistência + Firmenza, threshold 2+). If you fail the test, you act on the Passion. |
| **6-8** | Dominance | **Drive Condition** active. You cannot avoid the Passion's influence. Certain actions are **locked** (see individual Passion effects below). You gain access to **heightened states** when acting on the Passion. |
| **9-10** | Enslavement | The Passion defines your character. You can only act through it. The only way out is **Confession** (-2 immediately) + **Penance** + **Communion**. |

### Passion Locks (at 6-8)

| Passion | Lock (you CANNOT...) |
|---|---|
| **Gula** | ...refuse to consume what is offered. You must eat, drink, take, hoard. You cannot fast or share freely. |
| **Luxúria** | ...treat a person as a person. You see them as means to an end — pleasure, leverage, conquest. You cannot form genuine connection. |
| **Avareza** | ...spend, give, or risk resources willingly. You cannot make a generous gift or an uncalculated risk. |
| **Ira** | ...initiate diplomatic or conciliatory actions. You must confront or withdraw. You cannot negotiate in good faith. |
| **Tristeza** | ...accept consolation, hope, or the possibility of improvement. You cannot inspire others or act with optimism. |
| **Acídia** | ...begin a spiritual or devotional practice. You cannot pray, meditate, or participate in liturgy. |
| **Vainagloria** | ...act virtuously without an audience. If no one is watching, you cannot perform the virtue. |
| **Soberba** | ...accept help, guidance, or correction from anyone. You cannot be taught, healed, or led by another. |

### Heightened States (at 6-8)

When acting **on** the Passion (not against it), you gain access to a heightened state:

| Passion | Heightened Access |
|---|---|
| **Gula** | You can consume anything — poison, fire, cursed objects — and metabolize its essence for temporary power. |
| **Luxúria** | You can read and manipulate desire in others — know what they want most and offer it. |
| **Avareza** | You can see the true value of anything — hidden wealth, secret prices, the blood cost of transactions. |
| **Ira** | Your strikes carry the weight of everything you've lost — each attack creates the Exposto condition on hit. |
| **Tristeza** | You can see the end of all things — predict the outcome of any action with eerie accuracy. |
| **Acídia** | You become immovable — cannot be forced, compelled, or pressured by any external force for one scene. |
| **Vainagloria** | Your performance is mesmerizing — anyone who watches you must make a test of willpower or be captivated. |
| **Soberba** | You can stand alone against anything — for one scene, you are immune to all conditions except your own Passion. |

### Passion Triggers

A Passion increases when:
- The character acts in alignment with it (narrative choice)
- A Scar Condition triggers the Passion
- A Failed action in the Passion's domain
- A week of in-game time passes with the Passion at 7+ (the cascade — see below)

A Passion decreases when:
- **Confession:** -2 immediately (requires a spiritual father, a temple, or an honest confrontation)
- **Penance:** -1 after completing a narrative act of contrition
- **Contrary virtue cultivation:** -1 after consistently acting against the Passion for a scene
- **Communion:** -1 after participating in the Liturgy of Creation with genuine intent

### The Cascade Rule

When a Passion reaches 7+, the **next Passion in the chain** gains +1 per week of in-game time. **However:** the cascade only triggers **once per Passion per resolution cycle.** Once a Passion cascades, it cannot cascade again until that Passion has been addressed — reduced below 7 through Confession, Penance, or contrary virtue cultivation.

### The Dominado State

When **3 or more Passions are simultaneously at 7+**, the character enters **Dominado** — a permanent condition that defines their character arc. The character becomes increasingly driven by their Passions and increasingly disconnected from their Motivo de Origem.

The only way out of Dominado is a **complete spiritual reckoning**: all Passions must be reduced below 7 **simultaneously** through an extended Retiro involving Confession, Penance, and Communion.

## 10. RESSONÂNCIA (RS)

### What RS Is

Ressonância (RS) is a **dynamic resource** — a measure of the character's alignment with the divine Song. It is spent to execute powerful Partituras and regained through spiritual engagement.

**Starting RS:** 2-4, calculated during character creation (see §21).

**RS Range:** 0-10. Most characters operate between 2-7.

### RS as Resource Gate

To attempt a Partitura, you must have **RS ≥ the Partitura's complexity rating**.

| Partitura Complexity | Required RS |
|---|---|
| Simple | 2+ |
| Standard | 3+ |
| Advanced | 5+ |
| Masterwork | 7+ |
| Transcendent | 9+ |

### Attempting Beyond Your RS

You can attempt a Partitura above your current RS by:
1. Spending 1 VP (reducing your condition capacity)
2. Rolling an additional RS test (Fé + Compostura, threshold = complexity - current RS)
3. If you fail the test: **Dissonance Forçada** — you cannot execute any Partitura for the rest of the scene, and your RS decreases by 1d4

### Spending RS

- **Executing a Partitura:** Costs 1 RS for Simple/Standard, 2 RS for Advanced, 3 RS for Masterwork
- **Collective Amplification:** Each participant contributes 1 RS to the pool
- **Transcendência:** Costs all remaining RS (you drop to 0)

### Gaining RS

| Action | RS Gain |
|---|---|
| Act of Mercy (genuine, costly) | +1 |
| Ascetic Discipline (sustained practice) | +1 |
| Liturgical Participation (full attendance, genuine intent) | +1 |
| Accepted Suffering (enduring hardship with peace) | +2 |
| Mystic Experience (genuine encounter with the divine) | +2 |
| Martyrdom (sacrificing yourself for others) | +3 |

### RS 0 — Catatonia

When RS reaches 0, the character enters **Catatonia**:
- Cannot execute any Partitura
- Cannot take Pesado actions
- Gains Acídia at level 3 immediately
- Lasts 1d6 days or until taken to a priest for Confession + Penance + Communion

### RS 10 — Divine Intervention

At RS 10, a character may attempt **Divine Intervention** (d100 table, GM discretion).

**After a successful Divine Intervention:**
- RS decreases by 2d4
- The character **cannot gain RS for 1d8 weeks**
- No other Divine Intervention may be attempted for **1d6 months**

This prevents RS farming and keeps Divine Intervention genuinely extraordinary.

### RS Is Not a Level

RS does not define your character's power tier. It does not gate access to disciplines, attributes, or non-magical abilities. A character with RS 2 who possesses a Masterwork Partitura through a grimoire can still attempt it — they just pay the VP cost and risk the RS test.

RS is a **resource**, not a rank.

## 11. COMBAT

### The Compasso in Practice

(See §5 for the full beat economy. This section covers combat-specific rules.)

### Declaring Actions

At the start of each compasso, all characters (PCs and significant NPCs) declare:
1. **What** they intend to do
2. **Against whom** (if applicable)
3. **What speed** it costs (Ligeiro, Médio, or Pesado)

The GM resolves actions in speed order.

### Attacking

```
Pool = Vigor attribute (Força, Agilidade, or Resistência)
Die = Combat discipline (Lâmina, Impacto, Alcance)
Roll → count successes → compare to defender's threshold
```

The defender's threshold is based on their defensive discipline and current conditions:

| Defense | Threshold |
|---|---|
| No defense (caught flat) | 1+ |
| Defensive stance (Defesa discipline) | 2+ |
| Defensive stance + shield | 3+ |
| Defensive stance + shield + Exposto condition | Reduce by 1 |

### Damage → Conditions, Not Numbers

A successful attack **creates conditions** on the target:

| Excess Successes | Condition Applied |
|---|---|
| Met threshold (no excess) | Desritmado — next action costs +1 beat |
| +1 over threshold | Exposto — next hit is undefended |
| +2 over threshold | Sangrando — VP capacity reduced by 1 |
| +3+ over threshold | Atordoado — skip next compasso reaction |

Multiple conditions from the same attack stack. If the target reaches 4+ conditions, they become **Comprometido**. If they reach a scene-ending condition, they are **Quebrado**.

### Weapons as Rhythm Profiles

Weapons are defined by their **tempo, function, and armor interaction** — not damage dice.

| Weapon | Tempo | Function | Speed | Armor Interaction | Condition Created |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Short sword | Ligeiro | Controle | 1 | Cuts through light armor | Desritmado |
| Long sword | Médio | Impacto | 2 | Cuts through medium armor | Exposto |
| Great axe | Pesado | Impacto | 3 | Shatters heavy armor | Atordoado |
| Spear | Médio | Alcance | 2 | Keeps armored foes at distance | Imobilizado |
| Bow | Ligeiro | Alcance | 1 | Pierces light armor at range | Sangrando |
| Shield | Reação | Controle | 0 | Blocks most attacks | — (prevents conditions) |

### Stances — Conditions, Not Modifiers

Characters may adopt a **combat stance** at the start of a compasso. Stances create conditions, not numeric bonuses:

| Stance | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Forte** (Strong) | Your attacks create one additional condition tier on hit. Your defense threshold is reduced by 1. |
| **Legato** (Flowing) | You can take one additional Reação per compasso. Your attacks create one fewer condition tier. |
| **Staccato** (Sharp) | Your Ligeiro actions can target any speed class. Your Médio actions cost +1 beat. |
| **Rubato** (Free) | You can change stance mid-compasso as Tempo Livre. Your attacks create conditions chosen from two tiers (your choice). |

Stances change **what you can do**, not how well you do it.

### Mass Combat (Brief)

When armies clash, each unit acts as a **Choir** (see §19). Unit commanders roll **Estratégia + Tática** to determine the unit's performance. Individual PCs can influence the battle through heroic actions (extended compassos) or by targeting enemy commanders.

## 12. MAGIC — PARTITURAS

### What Is a Partitura?

A Partitura is a magical score — a structured invocation of the Song of Creation. It has three layers:

| Layer | Options | Determines |
|---|---|---|
| **Mode** | Jônico, Dórico, Frígio, Lídio, Mixolídio, Eólio, Lócrio | The spiritual nature of the effect |
| **Interval/Chord** | Terça, Quarta, Quinta, Sexta, Sétima, Segunda | The shape of the effect |
| **Rhythm** | 4/4, 3/4, 5/4, 7/8, Polirritmia | The temporal behavior of the effect |

### Executing a Partitura

```
Pool = Ressonância (Espírito) attribute
Die = Magical discipline (Voz, Instrumento, Escrita, Gesto, Coletivo)
Roll → count successes → compare to Partitura complexity threshold

Cost: 1-3 RS depending on complexity (see §10)
```

### Interrupting an Execution

If the executor is interrupted (takes damage, gains Dissonance, loses their medium), the execution fails and creates:
- **Dissonance** condition on the executor
- **RS decrease by 1d4**
- The magical energy released creates a **wild condition** on the scene (GM's choice)

### Collective Amplification

Multiple performers can combine their efforts:

| Tier | Performers | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Individual** | 1 | Standard execution |
| **Ensemble** | 2-3 | +1 scale tier to the effect |
| **Choir** | 4-7 | +2 scale tiers; each participant contributes 1 RS |
| **Concerto** | 8+ | +3 scale tiers; the effect becomes scene-wide; each participant contributes 1 RS |

If any performer in a Choir fails their roll, the Conductor must make a raised threshold test. If the Conductor also fails, the entire execution collapses with **Dissonance Forçada** for all participants.

### Composing New Partituras

(See §3E for the full process: Research → Composition → Practice → Testing.)

A character can compose new Partituras during Retiro. The GM sets the threshold based on complexity. Success creates a unique Partitura tied to the character's Motivo de Origem. Failure creates a flawed Partitura with a condition attached.

### Divine Intervention (RS 10)

At RS 10, a character may roll d100 for Divine Intervention. Results range from minor miracles to transcendent events that reshape the scene. After a successful intervention, the character cannot gain RS for 1d8 weeks and no further intervention is possible for 1d6 months.

## 13. SOCIAL ENCOUNTERS

Social encounters use the **same resolution engine** as combat — attributes, disciplines, thresholds, and conditions. The difference is the **stakes**.

### Social Stakes

Social encounters resolve conflicts of:
- **Persuasion** (convincing someone to act)
- **Negotiation** (reaching an agreement)
- **Intimidation** (forcing compliance through fear)
- **Reputation** (leveraging faction standing)
- **Reading** (understanding hidden motives, lies, emotional states)

### Social Compasso

A social scene is also divided into compassos. Each compasso represents one exchange of dialogue, one maneuver, one play in the social game.

| Speed | Social Action |
|---|---|
| **Reação** | Interrupt, counter-argue, read a micro-expression |
| **Ligeiro** | Make a point, ask a probing question, shift tone |
| **Médio** | Deliver a prepared argument, present evidence, make a demand |
| **Pesado** | Reveal a devastating secret, issue an ultimatum, burn a bridge permanently |

### Social Disciplines

| Discipline | Die Type Based On | Used For |
|---|---|---|
| **Retórica** | Discipline rating | Formal argument, public performance, debate |
| **Negociação** | Discipline rating | Deal-making, contractual interaction, leverage |
| **Reputação** | Discipline rating | Faction standing, social positioning, network access |
| **Intimidação** | Discipline rating | Pressure, dominance, social combat through fear |
| **Leitura** | Discipline rating | Reading people, situations, rooms, hidden truths |

### Social Conditions

Social encounters create conditions just like combat:

| Excess Successes | Social Effect |
|---|---|
| Met threshold | Minor shift — the target is slightly more receptive |
| +1 over threshold | Create a social advantage — the target reveals something they didn't intend to |
| +2 over threshold | Create a condition — the target gains Desritmado (next social action costs more effort) or Silenciado (cannot respond effectively) |
| +3+ over threshold | Major shift — the target's faction standing shifts, or a relationship changes permanently |

### Honra (Honor) — 0-5 Scale

Honra represents perceived integrity (consistency, not morality).

| Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| **5** | Impeccable reputation; advantage on trust tests (can use Reputação to automatically succeed social compassos that require trust) |
| **4** | Respected word; can add +1 to the threshold of any social action you take |
| **3** | Neutral — no mechanical effect |
| **2** | Questionable; your social actions create one additional condition on you (you must work harder to be believed) |
| **1** | Dishonored; doors closed; you need intermediaries for formal social actions |
| **0** | Infamy; expelled from communities; you cannot initiate social actions without an intermediary |

### IP (Influence Points)

IP represents political capital with a specific faction.

| IP Range | Status |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Conhecido (Known) |
| 4-6 | Aliado (Ally) |
| 7-9 | Confiável (Trusted) |
| 10-12 | Indispensável |
| 13-15 | Liderança (Leadership) |
| 16+ | Autoridade (Authority) |

Gaining and losing IP is governed by faction reactions and player actions (see §20).

## 14. KNOWLEDGE DISCIPLINES

Knowledge disciplines are given **equal mechanical weight** to combat and magic disciplines.

### Grimório

**Die Type:** Discipline rating (0-5 → d4-d12)
**Attribute Pool:** Fé (Espírito) or Intuição (Discernimento)

**Used for:** Reading, reconstructing, or identifying magical effects, grimoires, and traditions.

| Roll Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| Success (threshold met) | Reveals hidden information about a magical effect, identifies a grimoire's type and content, or grants advantage on a related magical execution |
| +1 over threshold | Reveals the grimoire's hidden conditions or weaknesses |
| +2 over threshold | Identifies a lost tradition and grants temporary access to a forgotten Partitura (one use) |
| +3+ over threshold | Reconstructs a damaged grimoire fragment into a usable Partitura |
| Failure | Creates Dissonance — you misread the text, and your next magical execution is compromised |
| Critical Failure (all dice < 7) | Creates Dissonance Forçada — the grimoire's defenses activate, applying conditions to the reader |

**Extended Action:** Reconstructing a complete damaged grimoire. Target: 8-15 successes. Requires Retiro (weeks of study).

### Tática

**Die Type:** Discipline rating (0-5 → d4-d12)
**Attribute Pool:** Estratégia (Engenho) or Percepção (Discernimento)

**Used for:** Analyzing battlefields, predicting movements, planning strategies, assessing threats.

| Roll Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| Success (threshold met) | Grants the party one extra compasso of planning; reveals one enemy weakness or vulnerability |
| +1 over threshold | Creates Harmônico for the party — next collective action has +1 scale tier |
| +2 over threshold | Creates Exposto for one enemy unit — their next action is undefended |
| +3+ over threshold | Grants the party a free compasso of action before the enemy can respond |
| Failure | Creates Desritmado for the party — next action costs +1 beat |
| Critical Failure (all dice < 7) | Creates a tactical blunder — the enemy gains an unexpected advantage (GM's choice) |

**Extended Action:** Campaign-level strategic planning. Target: 10-20 successes. Requires Retiro (days of analysis). Success shifts faction standings, opens new fronts, or reveals enemy supply lines.

### História Harmônica

**Die Type:** Discipline rating (0-5 → d4-d12)
**Attribute Pool:** Convicção (Espírito) or Astúcia (Discernimento)

**Used for:** Identifying traditions, reconstructing lost knowledge, understanding cultural and magical context, unlocking hidden Partituras.

| Roll Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| Success (threshold met) | Identifies a musical tradition's origin, reveals hidden cultural context, or grants narrative opportunity (a contact, a location, a secret) |
| +1 over threshold | Unlocks a hidden Partitura tied to the identified tradition (one use) |
| +2 over threshold | Reveals faction secrets — hidden alliances, betrayals, or resources |
| +3+ over threshold | Reconstructs a lost tradition, granting the party permanent access to a forgotten discipline at 1 die |
| Failure | Creates Silenciado — you cannot communicate what you learned (the knowledge is incommunicable for the scene) |
| Critical Failure (all dice < 7) | Creates Dissonance — the tradition's protective enchantments activate, applying conditions to the researcher |

**Extended Action:** Archaeological research into lost traditions. Target: 12-25 successes. Requires multiple Retiros across different locations. Success can recover entire lost Partitura schools.

### Magi-Tech

**Die Type:** Discipline rating (0-5 → d4-d12)
**Attribute Pool:** Mecânica (Engenho) or Logística (Engenho)

**Used for:** Operating, repairing, or understanding magi-tech devices and arcane mechanisms.

| Roll Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| Success (threshold met) | Operates the device successfully, reveals its function, or identifies its power source |
| +1 over threshold | Reveals the device's hidden capabilities or override codes |
| +2 over threshold | Gains temporary control of the device for the scene (even if it's enemy-operated) |
| +3+ over threshold | Reverse-engineers a component, creating a one-use magi-tech tool |
| Failure | Creates Atordoado — the device malfunctions, and you skip your next compasso reaction |
| Critical Failure (all dice < 7) | The device explodes or activates dangerously — creates Exposto and Sangrando for the operator |

**Extended Action:** Reverse-engineering unknown technology. Target: 8-18 successes. Requires Retiro (weeks of study and experimentation). Success creates a permanent magi-tech tool or weapon with narrative weight.

## 15. CRAFTING

Crafting is an **extended action** measured in compassos (for simple items) or Retiros (for complex ones).

### The Three Stages

| Stage | Discipline | Attribute Pool | What Happens |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conception** | Tática or Grimório | Engenho or Discernimento | Understand what you're making. Research the design, gather the knowledge. |
| **Execution** | Appropriate craft discipline | Vigor or Espírito | Build it. Instrumento for musical tools, Impacto for weapons, Escrita for scrolls, Magi-tech for devices. |
| **Testing** | Appropriate discipline | Any | Does it work? Execute it in scene. If it fails, you learn what's wrong and can try again. |

### Thresholds by Complexity

| Item | Target Successes | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Simple tool | 5 | 1 compasso (minutes) |
| Standard weapon | 8 | 1 compasso |
| Magical instrument | 12 | 1 Retiro (week) |
| Enchanted armor | 15 | 1 Retiro |
| Magi-tech device | 18 | 2-3 Retiros |
| Unique artifact | 25+ | Multiple Retiros, possibly with grimoire research |

### Failure

- **Failed Conception:** You don't understand the design. Creates Dissonance. You can try again with Grimório research.
- **Failed Execution:** The item is flawed. It works but carries a condition (e.g., "this sword creates Desritmado on its wielder").
- **Failed Testing:** The item doesn't work as intended. You learn one thing that's wrong and can adjust.

### Success

A successfully crafted item has **narrative weight**, not numeric bonuses:
- A blade that creates Exposto on enemies it cuts (sharp enough to find gaps)
- An instrument that grants Harmônico on the first note of a collective execution
- A shield that grants +1 reaction per compasso when the wielder is defending others
- A device that reveals hidden magical signatures when activated

## 16. JOURNEYS & EXPLORATION

Journeys are **compassos of their own**. Each "beat" represents a day or week of travel, depending on distance.

### The Journey Compasso

A journey has 4 beats (days/weeks). Each beat, the party faces a **journey event**:

| Beat | Event Type | Discipline Used |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Navigation / Route-finding | Tática or Leitura |
| 2 | Encounter (friendly or hostile) | Reputação or Intimidação |
| 3 | Survival / Resource management | Logística or Resistência |
| 4 | Arrival / Discovery | Percepção or História Harmônica |

### Journey Conditions

Journeys create conditions on the traveling party:

| Condition | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Exposto** | The party is vulnerable to ambush or attack |
| **Pesado** | The journey is slow and exhausting; all movement costs +1 beat |
| **Desritmado** | The party has lost its way; next journey action costs +1 beat |
| **Imobilizado** | The party is trapped by terrain, weather, or enemies |

### Successful Journeys

A successful journey creates **advantages** for the party:
- Safe arrival (no conditions)
- Local knowledge (advantage on first social encounter at destination)
- Faction contacts (gain 1 IP with a local faction)
- Hidden discoveries (find a Partitura, artifact, or secret)

### Failed Journeys

A failed journey creates **conditions** that persist into the next scene:
- Arrive wounded (Sangrando)
- Arrive lost (Desritmado)
- Arrive hunted (Exposto)
- Arrive broke (resources depleted)

## 17. RECOVERY — COMPASSO DE PAUSA & RETIRO

### Compasso de Pausa

After a scene resolves, a character can take a **Compasso de Pausa** — a brief moment of recovery between scenes.

**What it does:**
- Clear **1 condition** IF the character engaged in an activity aligned with their Motivo de Origem or a positive Passion
- If the character engaged with a **negative Passion** (acted on Gula, Ira, etc.), they must make a test of willpower (Resistência + Firmeza, threshold 2+) before clearing a condition. If they fail, the condition remains.
- Takes **minutes of in-game time** — not a "short rest," just a moment to breathe

**What it doesn't do:**
- It does not recover VP capacity (that requires Retiro)
- It does not clear Passion-driven conditions without the willpower test
- It does not clear Scar Conditions

### Retiro (Extended Downtime)

A Retiro is **one week or more** of dedicated downtime. It requires infrastructure: a temple, a mentor, a safe space, a community.

**What it does:**
- Clear **multiple conditions** (number = Fé attribute rating)
- Address Passions through Confession (-2 immediately), Penance (-1), or contrary virtue cultivation (-1)
- Recover from **Quebrado** state (requires narrative resolution of what caused the Broken state, not just time)
- Craft complex items (see §15)
- Research grimoires and traditions (see §14)

### Vigília (Vigil)

A character can choose **Vigília** during Retiro — they stay alert, keeping **1 condition** but gaining insight or preparation for the next scene.

**Benefits of Vigília:**
- Gain advantage on the first action of the next scene
- Sense approaching threats during the Retiro
- Maintain awareness of faction movements

**Cost of Vigília:**
- 1 condition remains uncleared
- Cannot address Passions during this Retiro

### The Theological Framework

Recovery in Qamareth is not "sleep for 8 hours." It is **spiritual engagement**. A character who rests without meaning — who sleeps through their Retiro, who takes Pausa without reflecting — gains nothing. The system rewards characters who engage with their Motivo, their community, their faith, and their flaws.

## 18. DISEASE, POISON & ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS

Diseases, poisons, and hazards are **conditions with stages**.

### Stage Progression

| Stage | Effect | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Initial** | Minor condition (Desritmado or similar). Easily treated with Compasso de Pausa. | 1-3 scenes |
| **Advanced** | Significant condition (Sangrando or worse). Requires extended healing action (§14 disciplines). | 1 Retiro |
| **Critical** | Character-threatening. Requires Retiro + appropriate discipline + narrative resolution. | Multiple Retiros |

### Passion-Linked Diseases

Some diseases are tied to Passions:

| Disease | Linked Passion | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Wasting Sickness | Acídia | Progresses faster when the character refuses spiritual practice |
| Burning Fever | Ira | Creates Exposto on the sufferer; the fever makes them lash out |
| Despair Blight | Tristeza | Creates Tristeza condition at level 2; feeds on hopelessness |
| Shadow Rot | Soberba | Prevents healing from others; the disease isolates the sufferer |

### Treatment

Treating a disease or poison is an **extended action** using the appropriate discipline:

| Treatment | Discipline | Attribute |
|---|---|---|
| Liturgical healing | Voz | Fé |
| Harmonic healing | Instrumento | Ressonância |
| Surgical intervention | Magi-tech | Mecânica |
| Community healing | Coletivo | Empatia |
| Herbal/natural remedies | Leitura | Percepção |

Thresholds are based on disease stage: Initial (2+), Advanced (4+), Critical (6+).

### Environmental Hazards

| Hazard | Condition Created | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Extreme cold | Pesado — movement costs +1 beat | Prolonged exposure advances to Sangrando |
| Extreme heat | Desritmado — next action costs +1 beat | Prolonged exposure advances to Tristeza |
| Toxic atmosphere | Atordoado — skip next reaction | Magical or industrial |
| Magical radiation | Dissonância — next execution costs +2 beats | From unchecked magical energy |
| Social isolation | Acídia — cannot begin spiritual practice | Prolonged isolation advances to Vainagloria |

## 19. ALLIES & COLLECTIVES

### Allies as a Choir

Allies are treated through the **Collective Amplification** system (§12).

| Number of Allies | Tier | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Individual | Acts on their own compasso. Uses their own attributes and disciplines. |
| 2-3 | Ensemble | Each ally contributes 1 die to the party's pool on relevant rolls. |
| 4-7 | Choir | Each ally contributes 1 RS to collective magical executions. The leader can coordinate actions. |
| 8+ | Concerto | Scene-wide effects. The party can attempt actions impossible for individuals. |

### Leading Allies

Leading allies is an extended action using **Reputação** or **Intimidação**:

| Roll Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| Success | Allies follow your command. They act in your compasso. |
| +1 over threshold | Allies gain advantage on their next action. |
| +2 over threshold | Allies create a condition for the enemy (coordinated strike). |
| +3+ over threshold | Allies achieve a secondary objective independently. |
| Failure | Allies are confused or reluctant. They act one compasso late. |
| Critical Failure | Allies break — they flee, turn against you, or create a condition for the party. |

### Ally Loss

When an ally falls in combat or is otherwise lost:
- The leader gains a **Passion trigger** (usually Ira or Tristeza, depending on the relationship)
- If the leader has a **Scar Condition** related to loss, it may trigger
- The party loses the mechanical benefits of that ally's tier contribution

## 20. FACTIONS & CAMPAIGN ESCALATION

### Faction Standing Scale

| Standing | IP Range | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Aliado** | 10+ IP | The faction trusts and supports the party. Resources available. |
| **Favorável** | 7-9 IP | The faction views the party positively. Minor assistance available. |
| **Neutro** | 4-6 IP | The faction is indifferent. Standard interaction. |
| **Suspeito** | 2-3 IP | The faction watches the party cautiously. Social actions create additional conditions. |
| **Marcado** | 0-1 IP | The faction considers the party a liability or threat. Doors close. |
| **Inimigo** | Negative IP | The faction actively opposes the party. Encounters are hostile. |

### Faction Reactions

When faction standing crosses a threshold:
- **Moving up:** New allies appear, resources become available, new Partituras are discovered, Passions may decrease (the party has support).
- **Moving down:** Old enemies strike, resources become scarce, enemies gain advantages, Passions may increase (the party is under pressure).

### The Living World

Factions track **IP with the party** and act independently. Each Retiro, the GM rolls for faction actions:

| Roll | Faction Action |
|---|---|
| 1-2 | The faction advances its agenda — creating opportunities or threats for the party |
| 3-4 | The faction seeks the party out — with a request, a warning, or a demand |
| 5-6 | The faction is consumed by internal affairs — temporarily inactive |

### Campaign Arc Through Faction Pressure

Over the course of a campaign, the party's actions shift faction standings, which shifts the world:
- **Early game:** The party is unknown. Most factions are Neutro.
- **Mid game:** The party has allies and enemies. The world responds to their choices.
- **Late game:** The party is a major force. Factions align against or with them. The final confrontation is not a boss fight — it's a **faction war** in which the party must choose sides.

## 21. CHARACTER CREATION — THE 20 QUESTIONS

Characters are created through **20 Questions** — a structured interview that produces a complete, mechanically valid character sheet.

### The Five Stages

| Stage | Questions | Mechanical Output |
|---|---|---|
| **Origem** (Origin) | Q1-Q4 | Attribute array, primary attribute assignment, childhood Passion |
| **Formação** (Formation) | Q5-Q8 | Primary discipline (3 dice), secondary (2 dice), tertiary (1 die), specialization |
| **Harmonia** (Harmony) | Q9-Q12 | RS calculation, Ressonância assignment, Partitura access, musical identity |
| **Conflitos** (Conflicts) | Q13-Q16 | Faction standing, grimoire hook, rival/ally creation, Honra |
| **Destino** (Destiny) | Q17-Q20 | Passions elevation, Motivo de Origem, final narrative synthesis |

### The 20 Questions

#### Stage 1: Origem (Q1-Q4)

**Q1 — Onde você nasceu?**
*Where were you born?*
Defines: Region of origin, cultural traits, starting contacts.
→ Determines primary attribute assignment (which attribute gets the highest die in your chosen array).

**Q2 — Qual era a ocupação da sua família?**
*What was your family's occupation?*
Defines: Professional familiarity, starting contacts, practical knowledge.
→ Determines secondary attribute assignment and starting equipment.

**Q3 — Como foi sua infância?**
*How was your childhood?*
Defines: Generates first Passion at level 2-3.
→ Maps to a specific Passion (see Passion table below).

**Q4 — Que evento marcou sua juventude?**
*What event marked your youth?*
Defines: A tragedy, discovery, revelation, loss, or unexpected triumph.
→ Determines your first faction relationship (favorable or hostile).

#### Stage 2: Formação (Q5-Q8)

**Q5 — Quem foi seu mentor?**
*Who was your mentor?*
Defines: School of Formation, available disciplines, contact network.
→ Determines your primary discipline and its starting die type.

**Q6 — O que você aprendeu primeiro?**
*What did you learn first?*
Defines: The first discipline you mastered.
→ Primary discipline set at 3 dice.

**Q7 — Você teve acesso à educação formal?**
*Did you have access to formal education?*
Options: Academia, Templo, Aprendizado informal, Autodidata.
→ Determines number of starting Partituras and RS calculation.

**Q8 — Qual habilidade você domina melhor?**
*What skill do you master best?*
Defines: Your specialization — intense focus within one discipline.
→ Grants +1 die to your secondary discipline.

#### Stage 3: Harmonia (Q9-Q12)

**Q9 — Quando você percebeu que o mundo canta?**
*When did you realize the world sings?*
Options: Desde criança, Durante o treinamento, Em um evento marcante, Ainda não compreendo completamente.
→ RS: +2, +1, +1, +0 respectively.

**Q10 — Você foi treinado para usar magia?**
*Were you trained to use magic?*
Options: Treinamento formal, Aprendizado fragmentado, Tradição familiar, Nenhum treinamento.
→ RS: +1, +1, +0, +0. Determines starting Partitura count: 3, 2, 2, 1.

**Q11 — Qual tradição musical mais influenciou você?**
*Which musical tradition most influenced you?*
Options: Litúrgica, Marcial, Erudita, Natural, Popular.
→ RS: +1 if Litúrgica or Erudita. Defines dominant Mode for your Partituras.

**Q12 — Qual som define sua memória mais forte?**
*What sound defines your strongest memory?*
Defines: Musical identity and possible secondary Motif.
→ Determines your Ressonância attribute die quality.

#### Stage 4: Conflitos (Q13-Q16)

**Q13 — Quem você decepcionou?**
*Who did you disappoint?*
Defines: A rival, enemy, or frustrated mentor.
→ Creates Rival NPC.

**Q14 — Quem confia em você?**
*Who trusts you?*
Defines: An ally who can help in critical moments.
→ Creates Ally NPC.

**Q15 — Qual lei você já quebrou?**
*What law have you broken?*
Options: Contrabando, Heresia, Espionagem, Deserção, Rebelião.
→ Defines initial relationship with authorities. Affects Honra.

**Q16 — Qual facção conhece seu nome?**
*Which faction knows your name?*
→ +1 IP with that faction. Defines Honra starting value (3-5 based on Q15 + faction reputation).

#### Stage 5: Destino (Q17-Q20)

**Q17 — Qual foi sua maior vitória?**
*What was your greatest victory?*
Defines: Generates a positive Passion at level 2-3.
→ Maps to a specific Passion (see Passion table below).

**Q18 — Qual foi seu maior fracasso?**
*What was your greatest failure?*
Defines: Generates a negative Passion at level 2-3.
→ Maps to a specific Passion.

**Q19 — Por que você continua lutando?**
*Why do you keep fighting?*
Options: Fé, Liberdade, Conhecimento, Família, Poder, Redenção, Justiça, Vingança.
→ Defines your Drive — the narrative engine that fuels advancement.

**Q20 — Qual é a nota que define sua alma?**
*What is the note that defines your soul?*
Options: Esperança, Vingança, Devoção, Curiosidade, Liberdade, Ambição, Redenção, Proteção, Justiça, Conhecimento.

## 22. ECONOMY & TRADE

### Condition-Based Markets

Trade is not a spreadsheet. It is **narrative pressure** that creates opportunities and constraints.

### Trade Route Conditions

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Seguro** (Secure) | Goods flow freely. Prices are stable. No mechanical effect. |
| **Ameaçado** (Threatened) | Bandits, weather, or faction conflict disrupt trade. Prices fluctuate. Characters with Logística gain advantage on navigation. |
| **Bloqueado** (Blocked) | The route is closed. Goods cannot pass. Characters must find alternatives or negotiate passage. |

### Economic Actions

Economic interactions use disciplines, not gold-counting:

| Action | Discipline | Attribute |
|---|---|---|
| Negotiate a deal | Negociação | Manipulação or Comando |
| Leverage faction standing | Reputação | Comando or Etiqueta |
| Apply economic pressure | Intimidação | Comando |
| Assess market conditions | Logística | Estratégia |
| Identify black market contacts | Leitura | Astúcia |

### Economic Warfare

Factions can wage economic war on each other (and on the party):

| Tactic | Discipline Used | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Sabotage | Tática + Logística | Creates Bloqueado on a rival's trade route |
| Counterfeiting | Mecânica + Manipulação | Creates economic instability (prices fluctuate wildly) |
| Bribery | Negociação + Manipulação | Shifts an NPC's faction standing |
| Rumor-spreading | Retórica + Manipulação | Creates conditions on a faction's reputation (Suspeito or Marcado) |

### Wealth as Narrative, Not Numbers

Characters do not track individual coins. Wealth is expressed as:
- **Access:** Can you afford what you need? (Yes/No, based on Honra, faction standing, and Logística)
- **Debt:** Do you owe someone? (Creates a condition: "Marcado by the Merchant Lords of Olynn")
- **Gifts:** Can you afford to be generous? (Spend wealth to gain IP, reduce a Passion, or clear a condition)

### Inflation and Deflation

The GM tracks the macro-economy during Retiros:

| Economic State | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Deflation** | Prices drop. Goods are cheap but jobs are scarce. Creates Tristeza. |
| **Stability** | Normal trade. No mechanical effect. |
| **Inflation** | Prices rise. Goods are expensive but jobs are plentiful. Creates Gula. |
| **Hyperinflation** | Currency is collapsing. Barter replaces coin. Creates Avareza and Ira. |
